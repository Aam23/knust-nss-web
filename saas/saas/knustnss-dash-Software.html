Software Developer

Software Development Division
UITS, KNUST
Deadline : 1st August 2024, 11:59 PM
Time : 11:59 PM
Role Description

As a Software Developer, your primary responsibility is to design, develop, and maintain software applications that meet specific user needs and business requirements. You will work closely with cross-functional teams to understand project objectives and translate them into functional, efficient, and reliable code. Utilizing your programming expertise, you will contribute to the entire software development lifecycle, from concept and design to testing, implementation, and ongoing support.

Responsibilities

Provided below are the responsibilities of a Software Developer:

  • Collaborate with cross-functional teams to design, develop, and deploy software applications that meet business requirements and user needs
  • Keep up-to-date with the latest industry trends and best practices to contribute fresh ideas to the development process
  • Participate in the entire software development lifecycle, from planning and design to testing and implementation
  • Troubleshoot and resolve issues, identify bugs, and continuously improve the software's functionality
  • Work on integrating and customizing CMS, e-Learning Platforms like Moodle, Library Management Systems, WordPress, and Drupal to enhance the user experience
  • Write clean, efficient, and maintainable code, ensuring high performance and scalability of the software solutions
Skill & Experience
  • Basic knowledge of operating systems such as Windows and Linux. Knowledge in administering Mac is a plus but not required
  • Cloud computing skills: Familiarity with cloud applications like Office365, Google Cloud Platform, AWS, and Microsoft Azure is a plus but not required
  • Communication and interpersonal skills
  • Familiarity with hardware: Working with physical devices—such as servers or printers—is an important part of a system administrator's job
  • Knowledge of networks: Being able to set up and maintain Local Area Networks (LAN) and Wide Area Networks (WAN)
